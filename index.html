Youth mental health response team coming to Acadiana

A new mobile response team tasked with assisting youth and their families in moments of crisis will be hitting the streets this summer. After a lengthy selection and training process, Lafayette based nonprofit The Extra Mile will be running the program, which is part of an effort by the Louisiana Department of Health to expand resources for youth experiencing mental health challenges. “The families that we serve have needed this for a long time,” said Evangeline Blanchard, director of the organization’s Family Resource Center and Home Builders program, which assists families whose children are at risk of being placed in foster care or are being reunified after they were in foster care.
